SDE v0.8 — Structural Replacement Tier System
This document defines SDE v0.8 as a standalone evaluative framework. It maintains the anti-inflation capped scoring system of v0.7 (max VII = 120), while introducing Structural Replacement Tiers (SR-Tiers) as qualitative differentiation within the capped range.
I. Core Principle
SDE v0.8 preserves mathematical stability. The Value Inversion Index (VII) remains capped at 120. No textual escalation may exceed this ceiling. Instead of expanding numeric range, v0.8 introduces qualitative tier labeling.
II. Value Inversion Index (VII)
Maximum Score: 120
Components:
1. Prior Legitimacy Source Identification (20)
2. Hierarchy Inversion Clarity (40)
3. Negation Intensity (20)
4. Replacement-Source Clarity (40)
Score Ranges:
0–40 Reform Level
40–70 Moral Tension
70–90 Radical Reversal
90–110 Civilizational Reversal
110–120 Structural Replacement
III. Structural Replacement Tier (SR-Tier)
When VII falls within 110–120, the text qualifies as Structural Replacement. SR-Tier differentiates the type of replacement without increasing numerical score.
SR-1: Institutional Replacement
    Political or governance restructuring. Example: constitutional replacement or sovereignty transfer.
SR-2: Civilizational Replacement
    Reordering of civilizational legitimacy or foundational myth structures.
SR-3: Ontological Replacement
    Replacement grounded in species identity, metaphysical identity, or ontological superiority claims.
SR-4: Absolute Moral Replacement
    Replacement justified by universal moral absolutes, divine mandate, or eschatological authority.
IV. Anti-Inflation Doctrine
SDE v0.8 prohibits score inflation. Qualitative differentiation occurs through SR-Tier labeling only. This ensures model stability across political, civilizational, and ontological texts.
V. Operational Rule
If VII < 110 → No SR-Tier assigned.
If VII 110–120 → Assign SR-Tier (1–4).
SR-Tier does not modify VII score.
VI. System Integrity
SDE v0.8 maintains:
- Numerical ceiling stability
- Cross-domain applicability
- Risk-type differentiation
- Compatibility with institutional analysis
Conclusion
SDE v0.8 upgrades structural classification without altering numeric architecture. It is a stable, independently operable evaluative framework.
